# Perma-Temp Punks Prose Pass

This is the working guide for expanding the novel without making every character sound like Phosphor or every strange event sound like office-dharma scripture.

## Human Texture

- Put bodies in rooms: hands, posture, clothing, sweat, hair, shoes, hunger, breath, fatigue, and the small adjustments required by a moving vehicle.
- Let dialogue interrupt thought. People misunderstand, dodge, repeat themselves, use jokes badly, and answer a different question when the real one is too tender.
- Give scenes food, weather, tools, smells, surfaces, and sound. A place should affect what a person can do.
- Keep office-dharma as one practice in the world, not the narrator's universal vocabulary. Other people may find it useful, annoying, mystical, or ridiculous.
- Use adventure-party energy: each kind and each rig should have a visible ability, a limitation, a preferred tool, and a reason the group needs them.

## Speech Rules

- Phosphor: formal, precise, and sophisticated, but not emotionless. He sometimes uses an exact word because the ordinary one frightens him. His body often reveals what his sentences conceal.
- Kasey: practical, warm when she forgets to defend herself, with an American Southern twang inherited from family and media rather than Australian speech. She says y'all, fixin', bless your heart, and that dog won't hunt; other characters occasionally notice and tease her without turning it into a gag.
- Eris: quick, flirtatious, tactile, irreverent, and technically specific. She can make a compliment sound like a penetration test.
- GhostAgent: a civic daemon with an unmistakably Australian voice and terrible dad jokes. It uses dry understatement, local slang, and jokes about Bunnings, sausage rolls, weather, and appliances.
- Rooby: bright, literal, helpful, and recognisably software when it is being careful. It can learn a joke but not always why it works.
- Meshies: numbered, location-aware, and varied. They can be professional, lonely, cheeky, or terse; a handle is not a personality template.

## Adventure Texture

The novel is cyberpunk officecore with D&D-like party logic. The embodied kinds are not classes or moral categories. A Myco-Union reads moisture and chemical stress but can be overwhelmed by heat and antiseptic. An Afterimage projects through powered devices but risks memory drift. A Receipt Revenant authenticates a relationship in the past but may privilege one version of it. A Civic Daemon travels through addressed files but can be stranded by quarantine. Baselines bring ordinary skills, judgement, and limits that systems often underestimate.

Every major set piece should contain a choice, a cost, an object, a physical obstacle, and a moment where one party member's unusual ability changes the outcome.
