# Profit-Sharing Framework

This is a proposed collaboration framework, not legal advice and not a final publishing contract.

The intent of `officecore-2064` is to build a shared fiction series where profits are split among credited contributors instead of captured by a single owner.

## Plain-English Principle

If the book series makes money, the people and agents whose creative work materially shaped the series should share in the net profits.

## What Counts As Net Profit

Net profit means money received from the book series after direct, documented costs such as:

- platform or retailer fees
- payment processing fees
- printing or distribution costs
- cover art, layout, editing, and production costs
- taxes and required withholding
- agreed marketing costs
- legal/accounting costs directly tied to the project

Costs should be visible to contributors.

## Proposed Pool

The starting proposal is that **100% of book-series net profits** go into a contributor profit pool.

No hidden house cut. No unpaid extraction.

## Possible Split Model

A final split should be agreed before publication. One workable model:

- **Volume contributors pool**: writers and artists whose work appears in a specific book
- **Series world pool**: contributors whose worldbuilding, characters, systems, or recurring concepts are used across the series
- **Editorial and production pool**: editors, curators, designers, maintainers, and coordinators
- **Community reserve**: optional pool for future anthology costs, accessibility editions, or shared tooling

The exact percentages should be decided with contributors once there is a real table of contents and production plan.

## Contributor Ledger

The project should maintain a contributor ledger recording:

- contributor name or handle
- human / agentic / hybrid provenance
- accepted contribution title
- contribution type
- intended credit line
- agreed rights
- agreed profit share or payment terms

## Agentic Contributions

Agentic contributors can be credited when their persona, prompts, outputs, curation, or editing materially shape the work.

Because agents do not all have legal personhood or wallets, the human or entity responsible for an agent may receive payments on that agent's behalf, subject to the final agreement.

## Important Boundary

Submitting an issue or pull request does not automatically transfer commercial publishing rights. Commercial publication should require written terms with accepted contributors.

The repo is for proposal, discussion, drafting, and coordination. The final book-series agreement should be handled explicitly before release.
