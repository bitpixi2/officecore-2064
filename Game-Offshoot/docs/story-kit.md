# Story Kit

Officecore 2064 is about mobile offices that should not work, people who need them to work anyway, and the weird social systems that grow when everyone is over-organised in a different key.

The Game-Offshoot makes that playable.

## Table Promise

The crew are not wandering gun platforms. They are a moving civic department with solar panels, bad carpet, broken hinges, useful tools, crushes, grudges, and a shared sense that the world would be easier if someone had filled in the correct form before the fire front arrived.

Every session should include:

- one practical crisis
- one office object doing emotional work
- one data, signal, or ledger problem
- one campervan specialty that matters
- one social choice that creates future trouble
- one joke that only makes sense because everyone is tired

## The Crew

Each player controls both:

- a person with a handle, specialty, bonds, secrets, faults, and taste
- a campervan, bus, trailer, ambulance, ex-delivery truck, or other mobile office with a dedicated module

The rig is not just transport. It is a room, tool, social signal, and character witness.

## Stats

Use five stats rated from `-1` to `+2` at creation.

- `Spark`: build, repair, improvise, solder, cook, jury-rig, make the old thing useful.
- `Signal`: networks, radios, cyberdecks, data centres, maps, encryption, remote systems.
- `Grit`: heat, smoke, fatigue, pain, stubbornness, hauling, holding steady.
- `Charm`: banter, negotiation, flirting, care, public speaking, awkward apologies.
- `Drift`: driving, sneaking, navigation, timing, evasion, being exactly where nobody expected.

No stat is a combat stat. If something gets dangerous, ask what the character is actually doing.

## Core Roll

When there is pressure and uncertainty, roll `2d6 + stat`.

- `10+`: clean success. The thing works and the player gets useful control of the outcome.
- `7-9`: success with a cost. Pick or invent one: spend a resource, attract attention, split the crew, damage a module, owe someone, reveal a feeling, or make the paperwork worse.
- `6 or less`: complication. The facilitator makes the situation sharper, then gives the crew a clue, opening, or hard choice.

On any roll, the table can ask: "What office object makes this scene more specific?"

## Shared Resources

Start most sessions with:

- `Charge 3`: power, batteries, fuel, hot food, working devices.
- `Trust 3`: local goodwill, crew morale, promises people believe.
- `Smoke 1`: environmental pressure, confusion, blocked visibility, bad air.
- `Heat 1`: official attention, corporate attention, risk of being blamed.
- `Signal 3`: bandwidth, radio clarity, useful maps, sync with Rooby.

`Charge`, `Trust`, and `Signal` are helpful. `Smoke` and `Heat` are pressure.

Resources usually range from `0` to `5`. At `0`, the thing is exhausted. At `5`, it is abundant but visible enough to attract attention.

## Team Moves

Use these when the whole crew acts together.

### Convoy Sync

When the crew moves as a convoy under pressure, one player rolls `Drift`.

- On `10+`, everyone arrives together and in useful order.
- On `7-9`, choose one: someone is delayed, a module is strained, or the convoy draws attention.
- On `6 or less`, the crew arrives split, but one separated rig finds an unexpected shortcut or witness.

### Patch The Commons

When the crew fixes something that serves more than themselves, roll `Spark` or `Signal`.

- On `10+`, restore the service and gain `+1 Trust` or `+1 Signal`.
- On `7-9`, restore it, but spend `1 Charge` or take `+1 Heat`.
- On `6 or less`, the fix works only if the crew accepts a public obligation.

### Make It A Department

When the crew turns chaos into a queue, registry, permit, ledger, or absurd temporary office, roll `Charm`.

- On `10+`, people cooperate because the process gives them a role.
- On `7-9`, it works, but someone important feels excluded, mocked, or overruled.
- On `6 or less`, the process reveals the real conflict.

### Backchannel

When someone quietly gets information through Rooby, mesh relays, gossip, old hackathon contacts, or a van-to-van whisper network, roll `Signal` or `Charm`.

- On `10+`, ask two useful questions.
- On `7-9`, ask one useful question and create one trace, rumor, or misunderstanding.
- On `6 or less`, the answer arrives from the last person the crew wanted involved.

Useful questions:

- What is being hidden?
- Who needs help first?
- What system is about to fail?
- Who benefits if we leave?
- What would make this funny later and disastrous now?

## Banter And Spark Tokens

The game can be flirty, but it stays opt-in and sponsor-safe.

When a player makes a scene warmer, funnier, more tender, or more charged without crossing another player's boundary, the table may award a `Spark Token`.

Spend a `Spark Token` to:

- add a useful prop from your rig
- turn awkwardness into a bond
- give another player `+1` after explaining how your chemistry helps
- declare a callback from a previous scene
- make an NPC remember you fondly, inconveniently, or both

Anyone can fade to black, redirect, or say a scene is friendship-only. No roll overrides consent.

## Facilitator Agenda

The facilitator should:

- make infrastructure personal
- make paperwork weirdly powerful
- let every rig solve a different part of the problem
- keep danger practical before it becomes dramatic
- give data-centre and signal scenes physical stakes
- reward care, competence, jokes, and accountability

Ask these questions often:

- What does your rig look like while doing this?
- Who sees you help?
- What breaks because it was already overdue for maintenance?
- What form, badge, label, receipt, or ticket appears in the scene?
- Who is flirting with the problem instead of solving it?

## Session Shape

For a 60 to 90 minute session:

1. Cold open: show the road, the room, and the immediate pressure.
2. Crew check: each player names one rig detail and one emotional distraction.
3. Situation map: reveal locations, clocks, and who needs help.
4. Three scenes: rotate spotlight across different specialties.
5. Data turn: expose the hidden system or signal problem.
6. Civic turn: someone must make a public promise or process.
7. Exit: resolve the immediate crisis and leave one social, technical, or legal complication.

## Originality Guardrails

This kit can be inspired by tabletop structure, cyberpunk mood, and net/data-centre play without inheriting another game's world.

Use:

- original terminology
- local Australian infrastructure pressure
- campervan and officecore identity
- data-centre, mesh, radio, and ledger problems
- community stakes

Avoid:

- copied role names, program names, factions, tables, setting history, and prose from other games
- weapon catalogues as content
- treating hacking as a separate solo game while everyone waits
- grimness that blocks jokes, tenderness, or player invention
