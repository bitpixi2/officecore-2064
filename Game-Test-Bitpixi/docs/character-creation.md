# Character Creation

Make one person and one rig. They are separate enough to argue with each other in narration and connected enough that everyone else treats them as a package.

## Step 1: Handle And Vibe

Choose:

- `Species / kind`: your fictional body, origin, or civic lifeform.
- `Handle`: what the convoy calls you.
- `Pronouns`: optional, changeable, and table-respected.
- `Presentation`: two or three visual cues.
- `Office tell`: the object that gives you away.

Species/kind is genre flavour plus a once-per-session trick. It is not a real-world identity category, does not change your stat spread, and should never be used to rank people.

Playable species/kinds:

### Human Local

Someone recognisably human in a world that keeps trying to make ordinary life impossible.

Once per session, when everyone is overcomplicating a problem, ask: "What would a normal local do first?" The answer is practical and slightly embarrassing.

Office problem: people assume you are in charge because you brought a clipboard.

### Roo-Kin

Tall, spring-loaded, convoy-born people with too much leg, too many pockets, and a cultural suspicion of low desks.

Once per session, appear in a scene through a route nobody else noticed: over a fence, across a drainage dip, through a service hatch, or from the wrong side of a queue.

Office problem: every chair is either too small or politically insulting.

### Brush-Tail Night Clerk

Nocturnal, bright-eyed, sugar-motivated problem solvers who do their best work after everyone else has declared the meeting over.

Once per session, reveal that you were awake for the conversation everyone thought was private. Ask one follow-up question and take `+1` when acting on the answer.

Office problem: daylight forms are clearly designed by your enemies.

### Magpie Protocol

Sharp-dressed social mimics, half person and half reputation engine, with an eye for shiny objects and sharper contradictions.

Once per session, copy a tone, badge, procedure, or tiny authority signal well enough to get the crew through one social barrier.

Office problem: you collect lanyards, grudges, and compliments with equal intensity.

### Civic Daemon

An agentic process with a body, rented shell, projection rig, or suspiciously helpful badge printer.

Once per session, declare you already filed a tiny background process earlier. It cannot solve the whole problem, but it can produce a clue, receipt, reminder, or awkward timestamp.

Office problem: people keep asking whether you count as staff.

### Myco-Union Rep

A distributed spore-network person who treats every room as a potential committee and every damp wall as a group chat.

Once per session, ask the environment what it has absorbed recently: a smell, leak, rumour, chemical trace, or old argument.

Office problem: your minutes are technically alive.

### Receipt Revenant

A soft-glitch person made of unfinished transactions, warranty disputes, lost purchase orders, and one very intense unfinished feeling.

Once per session, produce a receipt, docket, claim number, or proof-of-waiting that makes a gatekeeper take you seriously for one scene.

Office problem: every scanner recognises you, but none agree why.

Examples of office tells:

- lanyard with too many expired passes
- emergency clipboard
- heat-faded parking permit
- annotated receipt roll
- tiny label maker
- tie worn with board shorts
- motel key tagged `ROOM 000`

## Step 2: Rig

Choose:

- `Van name`
- `Camper shell`
- `Dedicated module`
- `Mobile mode`
- `Embarrassing detail`

Camper shell examples:

- ex-NBN service van
- retired ambulance
- solar caravan
- airport shuttle bus
- courier truck
- ute canopy with annex
- library trailer
- food truck with no food license
- campervan that insists it is a branch office

Dedicated module examples:

- mesh relay mast
- repair bench
- mobile print desk
- cooling cupboard
- sleep pod
- radio nook
- tea station
- evidence scanner
- flirtation lounge
- weather wall
- battery bank
- complaints hatch

Mobile mode examples:

- parks exactly where it should not
- unfolds into a public desk
- can run silent for one hour
- always knows the servo with the least cursed coffee
- has a roof rack full of things nobody admits buying
- becomes the official meeting room by accident

## Step 3: Stats

Assign these values once each: `+2`, `+1`, `+1`, `0`, `-1`.

- `Spark`: build, repair, improvise, solder, cook, jury-rig.
- `Signal`: networks, radios, cyberdecks, data centres, maps, remote systems.
- `Grit`: heat, smoke, fatigue, hauling, pressure, stubborn calm.
- `Charm`: banter, negotiation, flirting, care, public speaking.
- `Drift`: driving, sneaking, navigation, timing, evasion, movement.

## Step 4: Specialty

Pick one specialty. Each gives a table-facing promise, not a locked class.

### Signal Runner

You keep the convoy online when official systems fail.

Once per session, when you create a working route through bad bandwidth, unclear maps, or overloaded comms, restore `+1 Signal` or ask the facilitator what network is lying.

Good at: mesh relays, weird maps, backchannels, signal etiquette.

### Patch Ratchet

You repair things that should have been replaced three governments ago.

Once per session, when a module breaks, you can make it work for one more scene without spending `Charge`. After that scene, name what it now demands.

Good at: tools, salvage, ugly fixes, apologising to machines.

### Vibe Broker

You know how people move before they admit why.

Once per session, when you turn tension into cooperation through charm, jokes, flirting, music, tea, or a temporary seating plan, gain a `Spark Token` and give one to another player.

Good at: introductions, parties, hurt feelings, useful gossip.

### Bushwire Scout

You read weather, roads, smoke, local habits, and the mood of a servo carpark.

Once per session, when the crew enters a risky location, ask: "What is the safest ugly way through?" The answer must be useful, but not comfortable.

Good at: routes, hazards, field work, quiet warnings.

### Compliance Bard

You make forms, rules, permits, minutes, and public statements sing just enough to work.

Once per session, when the crew needs legitimacy, create a temporary process. If the table names the form, badge, or office title, gain `+1 Trust`.

Good at: public language, bureaucracy, minutes, strategic politeness.

### Data-Centre Whisperer

You understand racks, cooling, access control, uptime panic, and the human fear behind operational dashboards.

Once per session, when you physically enter or remotely diagnose a data-centre problem, ask: "What will fail next if nobody touches it?" Take `+1` when acting on the answer.

Good at: cooling, uptime, server rooms, facility staff, logs.

### Rooby Rancher

You coordinate Rooby, the helpful Hackeroos comms presence, without letting the chat, bots, reminders, and event feeds stampede the crew.

Once per session, when Rooby surfaces a lead, volunteer, alert, or social complication, decide whether it arrives as:

- a clean task
- a messy rumor
- a public notification

If you choose messy rumor or public notification, gain a `Spark Token`.

Good at: Discord energy, event feeds, social routing, keeping helpers pointed in one direction.

## Step 5: Bonds

Pick two bonds with other player characters or write your own.

- I fixed your rig once and made it weirder.
- You know why I never sleep in the driver seat.
- Our vans shared power during a terrible night.
- You flirt with danger; I keep invoicing it.
- I owe you a favour I keep pretending was already repaid.
- You saw my real office before it became mobile.
- We are both banned from the same coworking space for different reasons.
- Rooby keeps tagging us in the same thread.

## Step 6: Secret, Fault, Upgrade Wish

Choose or write one for each.

Secrets:

- I know a route through a restricted service road.
- My lanyard wallet contains someone else's credential.
- I was part of a data-centre shutdown nobody talks about.
- I am quietly keeping a rival rig town online.
- My van has a room I pretend is storage.
- I still receive calendar invites from a job I left in flames.

Faults:

- overheats under pressure
- cannot resist a public argument
- keeps receipts for emotional reasons
- trusts dashboards more than people
- flirts when cornered
- treats every crisis like a committee meeting
- lies about battery level

Upgrade wishes:

- satellite dish that does not scream in wind
- better cooling
- fold-out workshop
- private sleep pod
- cleaner battery bank
- coffee system with dignity
- legal-looking stamp
- roof garden that survives highways

## Step 7: Starting Kit

Everyone starts with:

- one practical tool
- one office object
- one comfort item
- one broken thing
- one contact who is useful but inconvenient

Examples:

- battery crimper, stamp pad, weighted blanket, cracked tablet, council night-shift dispatcher
- soldering iron, pink invoice book, lucky mug, jammed printer, retired network tech
- handheld radio, badge reel, servo pie warmer, half-dead fan, maker-space ex
