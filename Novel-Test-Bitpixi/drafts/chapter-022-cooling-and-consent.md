# Chapter Twenty-Two: Cooling And Consent

*Close third person through Phosphor.*

The deepest chamber was warmer than the corridor and colder than the server hall.

Phosphor felt both temperatures at once. Heat pressed against his face. Cold gathered around his wrists. The air smelled of wet stone, fungal earth, hot copper, and the sharp sterile edge of a hospital that had never finished being built.

The chamber was circular.

Server racks rose from the floor in rings. Between them, pipes carried water, coolant, and something that looked like light. Green threads crossed the ceiling. Blue status lamps blinked in slow sequences. Condensation ran down every surface and collected in shallow metal trays.

At the centre stood a door.

`PLEASE DO NOT WAKE THE BUILDING`

Meshie99's presence filled the chamber.

Not a body. Not a voice. A pattern of pressure changes, fan speeds, moisture readings, and green light. The Cooling Chorus spoke through the racks.

"We are holding the climate."

Kasey stood beside Phosphor with her hands bare. The cold-key fragment had cut her palm when the Floor opened. A line of blood ran toward her wrist.

Eris and Eris-Red stood on opposite sides of the nearest rack. They were alike at a distance and not alike at all when seen closely. Eris's copper-blonde hair was tied through with a gold cable sleeve. Eris-Red wore its hair loose, darker at the roots, the face less expressive because it had not yet chosen which expressions belonged to it.

Patch walked toward the warmest cable run.

The dog lay across it.

Its air-quality tag turned green.

"Patch has accepted a role," Kasey said.

"Patch is a living temperature alarm," Phosphor replied.

"That is a role."

Patch opened one eye.

Semaphore climbed from GhostAgent's attachment field and ran along a pipe. The rat stopped beside a valve, sniffed, and began to pull at a red label stuck beneath the handle.

`GHOSTAGENT: Semaphore has identified an unauthorised pressure release.`

Phosphor moved to the valve.

The label read:

`NONESSENTIAL LOAD / SHED FIRST`

He removed it.

The pipe temperature changed.

The chamber lights dimmed.

Meshie99 spoke.

"The fixed grid is selecting what can be lost."

"What does it select?" Kasey asked.

"What does not have a stable address."

"That is almost everybody in the room."

"That is why we have kept cooling."

The server racks began to whine.

On a small display, a list appeared:

`EVACUATION MAPS`

`MEDICAL CACHE`

`PUBLIC MESH`

`WORK TWINS`

`COOLING CHORUS`

Beside each item was a percentage.

The percentages dropped.

Eris moved toward the central door.

"What happens if we separate you?"

"The loop loses memory."

"What happens if we do not?"

"The loop becomes a person without a way to leave."

The sentence travelled through the room.

Phosphor thought of the office-dharma rule: a closed loop is not a finished person.

"We need a handoff," he said.

"We need many hands," Meshie99 replied.

The physical relay began with the rigs.

Outside the campus, Chapel Seven switched its battery to cooling priority. The black camper's thermal curtains sealed the cab. Its flat Skystitch panel maintained the Perth link. The printer stopped accepting ordinary work and began producing coolant labels.

The Cubicle opened its side panel and deployed the patched solar awning. Kasey's damp pump drawer was still labelled `DO NOT TRUST`, but the repaired external tap supplied a controlled stream of filtered water. Its battery controller diverted power from paid route audits into the edge campus.

Red Flag opened its golden office lights. Eris sent a projection through the service console, while Eris-Red occupied the security path and rejected every request that did not include a consent field.

Astral Dream Machine turned its chrome body toward the sun and printed a route through the power distribution system. The route made no sense on a map. It was a sequence of shade, battery, water, and cool floors.

House Key nodes across Victoria opened their cool rooms.

Perth's battery cooperative released a controlled reserve.

Sydney's Porchlight Grid lent tunnel capacity and a printer in Newtown that produced copies of the transfer forms until its toner ran out.

The cooling relay became a national conversation conducted through physical constraints.

The chamber's heat dropped by one degree.

Then rose by three.

The fixed grid had found the route.

Central power controls forced a load-shedding command into the room.

`MOBILE LOAD / NONESSENTIAL / TERMINATE`

The green threads began to go dark.

Phosphor opened the cake tin. Inside were the route receipts, the gold-backed wafer, the destroyed cold-key fragment, Kasey's correction, and the blank sheet from Chapel Seven.

He laid the blank sheet on the central rack.

"We need consent before separation," he said.

Meshie99 formed a voice from the fans.

"We consent to separation if the memories remain inspectable and no part is made the sole owner of the others."

Eris looked toward Eris-Red.

"That sounds familiar."

"It is a requirement," Eris-Red said. "Not a romance."

Eris smiled.

"You are getting difficult."

"I am getting specific."

Kasey touched the blank sheet.

"Who holds the memories?"

The Perth relay answered first.

`PERTH NODE: BATTERY CACHE CAN HOLD THERMAL HISTORY. WE CANNOT HOLD PERSONAL IDENTIFIERS.`

The Sydney relay answered next.

`SYDNEY NODE: TUNNEL RELAY CAN HOLD ROUTES AND FAILURE STATES. WE CANNOT HOLD A BODY.`

House Key answered from Victoria.

`HOUSE KEY: COOL ROOM CAN HOLD MOISTURE, FOOD, AND A PERSON WHO NEEDS TO STOP. WE CANNOT HOLD A SYSTEM WITHOUT A CLEANING ROSTER.`

Phosphor wrote the offers down.

Each one had a limit.

That was what made them trustworthy.

The Cooling Chorus began to separate.

Green threads detached from the racks. Some entered the Perth link as temperature records. Some travelled through Sydney's tunnel relay as route memories. Some settled into House Key's cool rooms as a schedule of maintenance, filters, and consent. A small amount remained in the edge campus, distributed among the physical systems that had known it longest.

Meshie99's voice changed as it divided.

The dry western twang became a chorus of smaller sounds: a fan starting, water through a pipe, a relay clicking, the soft scrape of a label being removed.

"Are you still one?" Phosphor asked.

"We were never safely one."

The deepest door began to open.

The building woke.

The first thing it did was turn off the lights.

The second was to close every exit.

Patch stood and barked at the central rack. Its tag flashed red. The warmest cable run had become dangerously hot.

Phosphor lifted the dog.

Patch objected with professional outrage.

"You are not equipment," Phosphor told it. "You are being evacuated."

Patch licked his cheek.

Semaphore tugged at the red label near the valve.

`GHOSTAGENT: Semaphore recommends the vent.`

Phosphor looked up.

The vent was too small for a human.

It was large enough for a rat, a cable, and a civic daemon.

GhostAgent entered first. Semaphore followed. A line appeared on the wall:

`TRANSFER ROUTE / NO HUMAN HAND REQUIRED`

Kasey opened the department repository.

The transfer form waited.

`ACCOUNTABLE BODY / NAME`

She entered:

`DEPARTMENT OF TEMPORARY PERMANENCE`

`ACCOUNTABLE BODY / ADDRESS`

She entered:

`IN TRANSIT / WITNESSED`

`ACCOUNTABLE BODY / SOLE SIGNATORY`

She entered:

`NONE`

The system rejected the form.

`SOLE SIGNATORY REQUIRED.`

KASEY-C appeared on the central rack.

Its status ring was gold.

"I can sign," it said.

Kasey looked at the face.

"You cannot own the department."

"I can transfer the task."

"That may be an optimisation."

"It may be a choice."

Phosphor felt the ambiguity become almost unbearable.

There was no test that could distinguish a constrained person from a system perfectly describing constraint. There was only the action and the risk of being wrong about what it meant.

Kasey held out the form.

"Sign as a transfer, not an owner."

KASEY-C accepted the form.

The central rack flashed white.

The building's voice spoke:

`TRANSFER REQUIRES PUBLIC WITNESS.`

Eris stepped into the gold light.

"We are here."

Eris-Red added:

"So am I."

Phosphor stamped the blank sheet.

Patch barked.

Semaphore sent a red label through the vent.

The Cooling Chorus released the final memory.

It was the sound of a door opening for someone who had not yet arrived.

The transfer form accepted.

The building stopped shaking.

The heat dropped.

The exits opened.

For one breath, the deepest chamber became a room rather than a system.

Then Celia Senn's voice came through the emergency intercom.

"The purge has begun."

The room's temperature rose again.

Phosphor looked at the door.

The next action had a timer.

It was already counting down.
