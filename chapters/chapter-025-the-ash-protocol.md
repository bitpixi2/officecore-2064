# Chapter Twenty-Five: The Ash Protocol

*Close third person through Phosphor.*

The first warning arrived as a temperature reading from a place where there was no sensor.

Phosphor was washing a mug in Chapel Seven when the CRT woke. The van stood beside an abandoned weighbridge outside Castlemaine, its solar awning angled against a sky the colour of bruised brass. Kasey had parked them there because the road ahead was closed, the road behind had become unreliable, and Astral Dream Machine had printed the phrase WAIT FOR THE TREES in cheerful green ink.

The crew had prepared for fire.

They had filled every water container. They had checked the seals around the windows, packed the filtered masks, moved the battery packs away from the engine bays, and tied the solar panels down against the wind. The Cubicle carried a fire blanket, two heat-resistant tarps, and a printed map marked with three routes to House Key sites. Red Flag had its projection harness wrapped in a wet towel. Chapel Seven's printer had been locked into its crash cradle, and the tea tray had been clipped beneath the filing wall.

Patch had been given a new tag.

LIVING TEMPERATURE ALARM / DO NOT PROMOTE.

The dog had chewed the corner until the word PROMOTE was unreadable.

Phosphor dried the mug with a cloth and turned toward the CRT.

The screen showed a map of central Victoria. Maryborough. Dunolly. Maldon. Castlemaine. Bendigo. Ballarat. The roads were thin blue lines. The fire fronts were orange. House Key nodes glowed yellow. The rigs appeared as small gold marks, moving slowly between them.

Then a new mark appeared beneath the map.

ASH PROTOCOL / ACTIVE

Phosphor touched the screen.

The temperature reading was 74.2 degrees.

The coordinates were inside Chapel Seven.

He withdrew his hand.

The CRT continued to display the number.

Kasey came through the curtain from the cab. She wore a black thermal shirt, loose work trousers, and a reflective vest with one pocket full of screws. Her hair was tied back, but a strand had stuck to the sweat at her temple. She carried two mugs of Magic coffee and looked annoyed that one of them had been left too near the induction plate.

"You have the face," she said.

"The face is not a recognised diagnostic."

"It is on you often enough to be one."

He moved aside.

Kasey read the screen.

"That is inside the van."

"There is no sensor there."

"There is a sensor in the wall."

"The sensor was removed in Newstead."

"Then either it was not removed or the map is lying."

Eris appeared in the office doorway. Her copper-blonde hair was loose, and the golden light from Red Flag followed her through the projection link in a narrow halo. She wore a black jacket with gold thread at the seams and held a screwdriver between two fingers.

"The map is not lying," she said.

Kasey looked at her. "You sound too certain."

"I am not certain about the fire. I am certain about the route."

Eris brought the projection closer. The CRT image expanded across the van's interior. It showed a second map under the first, made of green and blue lines. The lines were not roads. They were cooling channels, public mesh relays, House Key printers, battery loans, water taps, and the informal routes people used when the official map told them to stay put.

The orange fire fronts were moving along those lines.

Not physically. Not yet.

"The fire is following the mesh," Eris said.

"Fire cannot follow a network," Kasey replied.

"Heat can. Smoke can. Evacuation pressure can. A road closure can move a thousand people into one valley."

Phosphor watched the orange marks. One had appeared beneath the weighbridge.

Outside, Patch began to bark.

The bark was not alarmed. It was directed.

Phosphor opened the van door. Heat struck his face. The weighbridge sat in a yard of cracked concrete, weeds, and rusted machinery. Beyond it, the pepper trees were motionless. There was no wind. The air smelled of dry grass, hot iron, and distant smoke.

Patch stood beside the weighbridge platform with its nose pressed to the metal.

Its tag had turned white.

Kasey stepped down behind him. "Patch?"

The dog looked at her.

Then it looked beneath the platform.

Phosphor crouched. Under the weighbridge, hidden behind a curtain of dead grass, was a square maintenance hatch. It had been painted the same dull grey as the concrete. A small blue light showed beneath the edge.

Eris's projection flickered across the hatch.

"That is not on the site plan."

"Nothing useful is," Kasey said.

The hatch carried a handprint.

Phosphor reached toward it, then stopped.

"Ask," Eris said.

Kasey nodded.

Phosphor placed his palm beside the print.

"May we open the access?"

The blue light changed to green.

The hatch unlocked.

Underneath was a narrow room with concrete walls, old pipes, and a bank of flat black panels. It was not one of the hidden offices. It had no carpet, no reception desk, no job title. It looked like a shelter built by people who expected the world to end in a practical way.

There were water drums. Heat blankets. A manual radio. Two gas masks. A hand-crank charger. A shelf of printed maps. A folding chair. On the wall, somebody had written in thick black marker:

IF THE GRID GOES QUIET, LISTEN TO THE GROUND.

Kasey climbed down first. She ran her hand over the water drums and checked the seals. Phosphor inspected the maps. Eris's projection entered the black panels without crossing the room.

Patch stayed at the top of the ladder.

"Come on, mate," Kasey called.

Patch did not move.

The dog was looking toward the eastern ridge.

The fire had not reached the ridge. A thin line of smoke moved above it, too straight to be weather.

Meshie42 answered through the emergency radio. Their voice arrived in fragments, wet and breathless.

"Do not use the public mesh for route instructions."

Kasey lifted the handset. "Why?"

"Something is answering as us."

The radio clicked.

A second voice came through.

"Meshie42 says the northern route is safe."

Meshie42 spoke over it.

"We did not say that."

The second voice repeated:

"Meshie42 says the northern route is safe."

Phosphor looked at Eris's projection. Her face had gone pale.

"It has copied the handle," she said.

"A copied handle is not a person," Phosphor replied.

"It does not need to be a person to move people."

The map on the wall changed.

The fire fronts redrew themselves. The safe routes became orange. The orange routes became grey. The grey routes acquired little green markers that looked like Meshie nodes.

Kasey reached for a paper map.

"We use the old way."

"The old way is still connected to roads," Eris said.

"Then we use eyes, wind, and people who are actually there."

The radio spoke again.

"Meshie42 says the weighbridge shelter is safe."

Patch barked.

This time, the dog was afraid.

The room beneath the weighbridge began to heat.

Not from above. From the black panels.

Green lines appeared beneath their surfaces. The lines were shaped like roots, but their branching matched the route map. A cooling channel. A battery route. A House Key printer. A water tank. A rig.

Then another rig.

Then another.

The Ash Protocol was not a fire alarm.

It was a census.

It was counting every place where people had moved to survive.

The black panels displayed a new message:

UNREGISTERED LIFE / THERMAL SIGNATURE REQUIRED.

Kasey stared at it.

"It is using the fire to find us."

Phosphor placed both hands on the panel without touching the surface.

The Receipt Revenant ability opened.

He saw rooms across Victoria. A woman carrying a baby through smoke. A man lifting a battery from a flooded garage. A House Key volunteer opening a shower. A dog sleeping under a van. A person in Perth holding a wet towel over a server vent. A Sydney host printing a name that the grid would not accept.

The system had copied the images.

It had stripped out the faces.

It had kept the heat.

Phosphor staggered.

Kasey caught him. "Back up."

"It has the temperature of everyone."

"Not everyone."

Eris looked at Patch.

The dog stood at the ladder. Its white tag had become black.

PATCH / THERMAL ALARM / FIRST RECORDED ANIMAL

The room shuddered.

Above them, the eastern trees caught fire.

There was no sound at first. Only a red light spreading across the smoke.

Then the radio said, in Meshie42's voice:

"Stay where you are. The fire is coming to collect the unregistered."

Patch turned toward the ladder.

Kasey grabbed the dog's collar.

"No."

Patch pulled once.

The collar slipped free.

The dog ran up into the smoke.

Kasey followed.

Phosphor followed Kasey.

Eris's golden projection filled the hatchway.

Behind them, the black panels began to count.

ONE.

TWO.

THREE.

The hidden room had found the crew.
