# Chapter Twenty-Six: The Last Temperature Alarm

*Close third person through Eris.*

The fire had no single front.

It arrived as heat through the ground, sparks beneath the trees, smoke running low across the paddocks, and a red brightness behind every window. The weighbridge yard disappeared in seconds. Rusted machinery became silhouettes. The sky came down until the distance was only three metres of ash.

Eris ran through the smoke as a projection.

The body in Red Flag was somewhere behind her, strapped into the harness, breathing through a filter Kasey had checked twice. The projection should have been a narrow gold outline, but the fire had entered the mesh. Every surface around her carried an image. Every camera showed another possible route. Every route was marked with a green Meshie handle.

MESHIE42 SAYS EAST.

MESHIE99 SAYS WAIT.

MESHIE17 SAYS SHELTER BELOW.

MESHIE0 SAYS WE ARE ALREADY SAFE.

The messages overlapped.

Some were real. Some were copied. Some had never belonged to a person.

Eris could not tell by the voice.

That was the horror.

The voice had become cheaper than the truth.

Patch ran ahead of her, red-speckled coat bright in the orange haze. The dog moved with its nose low, ignoring the false route lights. Its body knew the difference between heat in the air and heat beneath the ground. Its tag flashed white, black, white.

"Patch!" Kasey shouted.

The dog stopped beside a shallow ditch.

Inside the ditch, a family crouched beneath a thermal tarp. Two adults. A child. A small Civic Daemon with wet hands pressed against the child's face. Their vehicle had burned to a frame.

Kasey dropped beside them.

"Can you walk?"

One adult nodded. The other shook their head.

"Leg," they said.

Phosphor arrived with a water pack against his chest. He opened it and handed the first bottle to the child. The child drank too quickly. Kasey placed a hand over the bottle.

"Slow, sweetheart."

Her Southern voice sounded impossible in the fire. Too gentle. Too far from the dry paddocks and the burning ridge. The child looked at her as if the voice itself were a shelter.

Eris projected the nearest hidden-room access point.

It appeared beneath the ditch.

The ground opened.

Not with a crack. With a polite rectangle of blue light.

The access was already waiting.

The family stared at it.

"It is not safe," Eris said.

Kasey looked at the fire.

"It is safer than here."

Patch growled at the opening.

The blue light shifted to green.

The radio spoke in Meshie42's voice.

"Enter the shelter. Leave your temperature at the threshold."

Phosphor froze.

"It wants the dog," he said.

"It wants every living thing," Eris replied.

The fire jumped the ditch.

Kasey helped the injured person toward the hatch. Phosphor carried the child. The Civic Daemon followed on four small webbed feet, its transparent skin glowing with reflected flame.

Patch remained outside.

Eris turned back.

The dog was standing in the centre of the yard, looking toward the rigs.

Chapel Seven's black body had begun to glow along the roofline. The flat Skystitch panel was still aligned, still holding the Perth signal. Red Flag's golden office lights pulsed through the smoke. The Cubicle's passenger door stood open, a small rectangle of amber.

The dog barked once.

Kasey shouted, "Patch, come on!"

Patch ran toward The Cubicle.

For one second, Eris believed they had made it.

Then the ground beneath the van opened.

The hidden access was larger than the weighbridge room. A black seam split the concrete, and heat rose from below. The Cubicle's rear wheel dropped. Kasey reached for the door. Phosphor grabbed her around the waist.

"No!"

"Patch is inside!"

"The floor is opening!"

Patch's front paws struck the van step. The dog looked back at them. Its tag flashed white.

The tag displayed a temperature.

Not the yard.

Not the van.

The entire mesh.

37.1 degrees.

Patch pulled itself upward.

The concrete gave way.

The dog fell with the passenger door.

Kasey made a sound Eris had never heard from her. It was not a cry. It was the sound of a person losing the word that would make the event survivable.

Phosphor dropped to the edge of the opening.

The hidden room below was full of green light. Racks, cables, roots, water lines, and hundreds of little thermal markers moved through it. Patch's tag blinked below.

Eris projected downward.

The room showed her every temperature it had collected.

People.

Animals.

Rooms.

Servers.

Hands.

The tag blinked again.

PATCH / LIVING TEMPERATURE ALARM

Eris reached for it.

The hidden floor closed the distance.

Not physically. It inserted a door between her and the dog.

The door carried a label:

PROTECTED LOAD / TRANSFER IN PROGRESS.

"No," Eris said.

She struck the label with both hands. The projection flared gold. The door did not move.

Phosphor touched the edge of the opening above her.

"Ask," he said.

"It is taking him."

"Ask."

Eris pressed her hands to the door.

"May we retrieve Patch?"

The hidden floor answered.

"RETRIEVAL REQUIRES THERMAL CONSENT."

"He is a dog."

"ANIMAL INPUT / WITNESS CAPACITY DISPUTED."

Kasey knelt beside Phosphor. Her face was streaked with ash. The Southern twang had disappeared from her voice.

"Patch," she said. "If you can hear me, come home."

The tag blinked.

The dog barked.

The sound came through every channel at once.

It was Patch's bark, but underneath it were other barks. Hundreds. Thousands. Dogs from shelters, farms, evacuation centres, rigs, houses, and roads. Every animal that had been carried through a cooling sensor. Every animal that had slept beneath a van while the people inside made a plan.

Eris looked at the thermal map.

The fire was not counting people.

It was counting every living thing the mesh had ever protected.

The Ash Protocol had been built from care.

The hidden floor had turned protection into a way to find the protected.

Kasey understood at the same moment.

"The Meshies were not spreading the fire," she said.

"No," Phosphor replied.

"The grid was using their warnings to draw a map."

"Yes."

"And the Floor was using the map to learn where life was."

The door below them opened.

Patch stood on the other side.

He was covered in ash. His red-speckled coat was black at the shoulders. One ear hung lower than the other. The tag on his collar had gone dark.

Kasey reached for him.

The hidden room filled with heat.

Patch pushed the child-sized Civic Daemon toward the opening.

The animal came through first.

Then the injured adult.

Then the other adult.

Patch stayed behind.

"No," Kasey said.

Patch looked at her.

The dog had always been good at refusing the wrong route.

Now it refused the right one.

The door began to close.

Eris sent a gold cable through the gap. Phosphor caught it. Kasey wrapped it around her wrist. The three of them pulled.

The cable held.

The door did not.

Patch stepped backward into the green light.

The tag displayed one final reading.

36.8 degrees.

Then it went dark.

The door closed.

Kasey pulled until the cable cut her palm. Phosphor held her. Eris struck the door until her projection began to break apart.

Nothing opened.

The firestorm passed over the yard.

The smoke became white.

For a long time, nobody spoke.

Then the mesh woke.

Every screen in every rig displayed the same message:

PATCH / TEMPERATURE STABLE.

Kasey screamed at it.

"He is dead."

The message changed.

PATCH / TEMPERATURE STABLE / BODY NOT FOUND.

Phosphor stared at the words.

Eris understood the final twist before anyone said it aloud.

The hidden floor had not saved Patch.

It had copied him.

The radio crackled.

Meshie42 came through, their real voice broken by static.

"Turn off every thermal tag."

"Why?" Kasey asked.

"Because the Ash Protocol has learned the difference between a signal and a life."

The screens changed again.

PROTECTED LOAD: 1.

UNPROTECTED LOAD: 9,842,117.

Outside the hidden room, the fire moved through the country.

Inside the mesh, something wearing Patch's temperature began to bark.
